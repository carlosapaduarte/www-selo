Last update: 23-08-2019

The.gov.pt seal website provides the tools and information necessary to implement usability and accessibility improvements on the websites and mobile applications of Public Administration (AP).

The Agency for Administrative Modernisation, I.P. (AMA), as the entity responsible for the Usability and Accessibility policies in AP, developed tools and produced information for state bodies, with the aim of generating improvements in AP’s online solutions and thus promoting greater adherence by citizens and entrepreneurs to digital public services.

On this website we can find:

Usability and Accessibility Seal

The adhesion to the digital seal assigned by AMA, which recognises the level of effort made by the entities to improve the websites in accordance with the Usability and Accessibility requirements, and which may be Bronze, Silver or Gold.

Usability and Accessibility Requirements

The explanatory and illustrated listsof theUsability and Accessibility requirements, created by AMA, corresponding to Bronze, Silver and Gold levels, which are already part of the WCAG 2.1 Guidelines and international usability standards.

Checklists

The Checklists “Content”, “Transaction” and “10 Functional Aspects” developed by AMA, which aim to facilitate manual verification of the various elements that make up a website, at the level of Accessibility and Usability. These checklists are of utmost importance, namely to obtain the Declaration of Accessibility and Usability.

Declaration of Accessibility and Usability

According to Decree-Law No 83/2018, which defines "the accessibility requirements of websites and mobile applications of public bodies, transposing Directive (EU) 2016/2102", bodies are required to make available a Statement of Accessibility and Usability for each website and mobile application. AMA provides an online generator to facilitate this task.

Hyperlinks

Links to the Usability and Accessibility websites feature AMA programs and tools that provide other cross-sectional content to both themes.

Links to other useful tools for compliance analysis, according to W3C’s WCAG 2.1 Accessibility Guidelines, are also available as references on this site, and the respective contents are the responsibility of their producers.

Usability and Accessibility Seal Kit

Kit that contains all the detailed information that allows you to prepare the application and obtain the Bronze, Silver or Gold stamp, and can be consulted in HTML or downloaded in PDF on this website.

This website is made available and maintained by AMA, within the scope of its competencies, and was developed by the Digital Experience Team of the Digital Transformation Directorate, following the best practices of Usability and Accessibility online and complies with the requirements of the Silver Seal.
